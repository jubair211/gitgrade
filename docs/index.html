The Problem

You think you studied programming for 6 hours this week. Your git history says 1 commit, on Sunday night.
Health Informatics students are learning to program while also studying medicine, data, and systems. It is easy to fall behind without noticing.
Most tools are built for experienced developers. GitGrade is built specifically for beginners in their 1st and 2nd semester.

What GitGrade Tracks

Weekly Habits

See exactly how many days you coded this week and whether you are building a real habit or cramming before deadlines.

Progress Tracking

Compare your commit frequency from week 1 to now. See if you are actually improving over the semester.

Coding Patterns

Find out when you code most and get honest advice about whether your schedule supports learning.

Beginner Milestones

Reach 10, 50, and 100 commits. Track your streak. Get encouraging feedback at every step.

Fully Automatic

No forms, no manual input. Just point GitGrade at your repo and it does the rest.

Honest Feedback

GitGrade tells you the truth. If you only committed on deadline day, it will say so - kindly but clearly.

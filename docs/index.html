The Problem

Beginners don't see their own patterns.

Health Informatics students are learning to program while managing lectures, clinical concepts, and data systems. Without feedback, it is easy to fall into deadline cramming without noticing — and wonder why skills are not improving.


GitGrade reads your git history and shows you the truth — automatically, in seconds.

Features
Everything a beginner needs to improve.

Five automatic commands. No configuration. Just point at any git repository.

📅

Weekly Habits

See exactly how many days you coded this week. Find out if you are building a real habit or cramming before deadlines.

📈

Progress Tracking

Compare your commit frequency from week 1 to now. See if you are actually improving over the semester.

🕐

Coding Patterns

Find your peak coding hour and get honest advice about what it means for your learning.

🏆

Beginner Milestones

Reach 10, 50, and 100 commits. Track your streak. Get encouraging feedback at every step.

⚡

Fully Automatic

No forms, no manual input, no account needed. Just run one command and see your habits instantly.

🔒

100% Offline

Reads your local .git folder. Your data never leaves your machine. No internet required.

CLI Commands
Five commands. Zero setup.

Point gitgrade at any git repository on your machine.

gitgrade scan .
Full overview: total commits, peak coding hour, and your first milestone check.
gitgrade habits .
How many days did you code this week? Are you consistent or a deadline crammer?
gitgrade progress .
Compare your first week of commits to this week. See your real growth.
gitgrade patterns .
Find your peak coding hour and get advice on your learning schedule.
gitgrade milestones .
Track your streak, total commits, and active coding days.
